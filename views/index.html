FAQs

We know our wedding is a little non-traditional and that you may have questions about expectations or even what to wear.
Don't worry we're here to help!

Can you tell me about the Hindu ceremony?

The Hindu wedding ceremony is traditionally conducted in Sanskrit and in the local language of the family. Shiv's family is from Hyderabad, India where they speak Telugu. Hindus attach a lot of importance to weddings and celebrations may extend several days. We will be having the abridged version at our ceremony, lasting about one hour. We'll be handing out a program before the ceremony to help you follow along as well as explain each ritual.

What should I wear to the Hindu ceremony?

Traditionally, ladies wear saris and men wear kurtas. This isn't necessary and a festive dress or suit is perfectly acceptable. Of course, bright colors are always encouraged!

Can you tell me about the evening ceremony?

The evening ceremony will be a non-denominational ceremony incorporating many meaningful elements. This event will kick off the night portion of the day.

What should I wear to the evening ceremony?

You'll be seated outside with shade on a grassy lawn overlooking vineyards. We encourage you to wear festive attire (be it Indian or western) and relax in the beauty of your surroundings.

What advice can you offer someone flying into SFO/OAK?

Sonoma and Santa Rosa are about a one hour's drive up north. If you plan on renting a car, let them know that you'll be crossing any number of bridges and will either need a Fastpass or money to pay the toll ($5-$7 depending on which bridge you cross). If you are flying in on a weekday, please take into account traffic, which could easily double the length of your trip.
